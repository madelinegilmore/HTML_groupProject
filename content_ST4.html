Causes of Endangered Animal Conservation

Protecting endangered animals is a key part of Sustainable Development Goal 15: Life on Land. However, many species are becoming endangered due to human activities and environmental changes. This page explores the main causes contributing to this global issue.

Habitat Loss Climate Change Poaching & Illegal Trade Pollution Overexploitation

Habitat Loss

Habitat loss is one of the biggest threats to wildlife. As cities expand and forests are cleared for agriculture and development, animals lose their natural homes. This makes it harder for them to find food, shelter and reproduce.

Climate Change

Climate change is affecting ecosystems all over the world. Rising temperatures and changing weather patterns make it difficult for animals to survive. Some species cannot adapt quickly enough, leading to population decline.

Poaching and Illegal Wildlife Trade

Poaching is the illegal hunting of animals for products such as ivory, fur and horns. This illegal trade continues to threaten species like elephants and rhinos despite laws being in place to protect them.

Pollution

Pollution, especially plastic waste, harms many animals. Marine life often mistakes plastic for food, which can lead to injury or death. Pollution also damages ecosystems and affects biodiversity.

Overexploitation

Overexploitation occurs when humans use natural resources faster than they can recover. This includes overfishing and excessive hunting, which leads to a rapid decline in species populations.
